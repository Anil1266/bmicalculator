BMR Calculator

What is BMR?

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) represents the number of calories your body needs to perform basic life-sustaining functions such as breathing, circulation, and cell production while at rest.

Why BMR is Important

Understanding your BMR helps you determine how many calories your body requires daily before considering physical activity. It forms the foundation for calculating your total daily calorie needs.

How BMR is Calculated

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, one of the most accurate formulas for estimating BMR. It considers age, gender, weight, and height.

BMR vs Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)

Your BMR represents calories burned at rest. To calculate total daily calorie needs, your BMR must be multiplied by your activity level.

Factors That Affect BMR

  • Muscle mass
  • Age
  • Gender
  • Hormones
  • Genetics

Frequently Asked Questions

Is BMR the same as metabolism?

BMR is part of metabolism. It refers specifically to energy used while resting.

Can I increase my BMR?

Yes. Increasing muscle mass through strength training can help raise your BMR.
